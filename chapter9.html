CHAPTER NINE

The Pain That Teaches

Nearly a year had passed since David started The Garden. Men found courage there. Stories surfaced there. Healing quietly took root there.

Life, however, carried another lesson. Growth and pain often travel together.

One morning David received a call from work. A project had gone terribly wrong. Mistakes caused delays. Money was lost. Frustration spread throughout the team.

Guilt immediately tightened around David's chest. Familiar thoughts returned. Old voices returned.

You should handle this. You should be stronger. You failed.

David sat alone that evening inside his apartment. Fear rose. Frustration rose. Pressure returned. Old habits waited nearby.

Silence suddenly looked tempting again. Hide. Carry everything alone. Pretend.

Then another voice surfaced. Aaron.

"Open rebuke is better than secret love."

David reached toward his phone and called.

"Aaron..." "I messed up." "I don't know how to fix this."

Aaron's response remained calm.

"You're being honest." "That's where healing always begins."

"Pain doesn't disappear." "Pain teaches." "Pain teaches best when truth enters the room."

David spent the next hours reviewing every detail. Mistakes became visible. Weak places became visible. Solutions slowly became visible too.

He accepted responsibility. He apologized. He asked for help.

Humility felt uncomfortable. Honesty felt uncomfortable. Truth often does.

Days later David sat with men inside The Garden. This time he shared personal failure openly. No masks. No pretending.

Silence filled the room afterward. One man finally spoke.

"I've hidden mistakes too." "Hearing your story gives me courage."

David looked around the room. Understanding suddenly settled inside him. Pain teaches more than personal lessons. Pain also becomes a bridge.

Mistakes never made him weak. Mistakes made him human. Real. Relatable. Honest.

Late that night David prayed quietly.

"God, give me courage to face pain openly." "Teach me through every difficult moment."

David understood something deeply now. Strength never meant avoiding pain. Strength meant allowing pain to teach, shape, and connect people together.
