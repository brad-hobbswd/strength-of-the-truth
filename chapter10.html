CHAPTER TEN

The Strength in Truth

Morning sunlight stretched across the city skyline as David stood near his apartment window. Life outside moved with familiar rhythm, though something inside him had changed completely.

Memories slowly surfaced. Bruises. Silent nights. Fear. Shame. Pain carried quietly through years of hiding.

David remembered Aaron's words. Truth first felt uncomfortable. Truth first felt painful. Truth eventually became freedom.

Hard conversations returned to memory. His father. Counseling sessions. Long nights. Difficult setbacks. Every step carried challenge. Every step carried growth.

David thought about The Garden. Faces filled his mind. Stories. Laughter. Healing. Men learning honesty. Men discovering strength.

Every life touched reminded David of something important. Survival was never the destination. Purpose waited beyond pain.

True strength does not hide. True strength tells the truth.

David sat near the table and opened his journal. Slowly he wrote familiar words across the page.

"Open rebuke is better than secret love." Proverbs 27:5

Those words guided him through darkness. Those words challenged pride. Those words revealed truth.

Love without truth felt incomplete. Truth without love felt harsh. Together both carried healing.

Later that evening David met with men from The Garden. Stories filled the room. Laughter filled the room. Honesty filled the room.

One man looked toward David quietly.

"You helped me understand strength differently."

David smiled. Warmth filled his chest.

"Strength never meant hiding pain." "Strength means facing pain honestly." "Strength means allowing truth and love to shape us."

Walking home beneath stars felt peaceful. Pain would still come. Life would still bring challenges. Hard moments would still arrive.

David understood something deeply now. Silence and healing never walked together. Truth and love did.

He whispered a final prayer.

"Thank You, God, for pain that taught me." "For truth that changed me." "For people who loved me enough to speak honestly."

For the first time in years David no longer felt merely strong. David felt whole.

Deep inside he understood a final truth. Love that speaks honestly, even when truth hurts, becomes the kind of love that saves.
