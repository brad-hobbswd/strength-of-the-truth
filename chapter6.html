CHAPTER SIX

Learning to Heal

The days that followed brought challenges David could not avoid. Healing demanded honesty. Recovery required action. Silence no longer offered shelter.

David sat inside the doctor's office and finally admitted everything. The lingering pain. Sleepless nights. Stress. Fear. Exhaustion. No minimizing. No pretending.

"You've ignored this too long."

The doctor's voice remained calm and gentle. "You need rest." "You need therapy." "You need time." "And stress cannot remain buried forever."

Emotion tightened inside David's chest. "I thought doing everything alone meant strength."

The doctor smiled softly.

"Strength isn't pretending you're invincible." "Strength means knowing when to accept help."

Later that evening David called Aaron.

"I scheduled therapy." "I started writing things down." "I actually listened."

Aaron laughed quietly. "Good." "Healing begins when pain finally receives a name."

Weeks passed. Recovery developed a rhythm. Physical therapy challenged muscles and strained healing ribs. Exercises felt frustrating. Progress often felt slow.

Yet small victories began appearing. Strength returned. Energy returned. Hope returned.

Healing also reached places beyond physical pain. David met with a counselor and slowly unpacked years of anger, fear, and shame. Childhood wounds surfaced. Old lies surfaced. Patterns surfaced.

Silence once felt safe. Pride once felt protective. Now both felt exhausting.

Aaron continued speaking truth through every difficult season.

"Healing isn't a straight line." "Some days will feel harder." "Honesty remains part of recovery."

Slowly David noticed changes. Pain carried less weight. Conversations felt easier. Joy appeared unexpectedly. Small victories suddenly mattered.

A day without discomfort. A conversation without tension. Laughter without guilt.

Healing no longer looked like becoming the man he once was. Healing looked like becoming someone new. Honest. Stronger. Willing to receive help. Free from shame.

One evening David sat beside the window watching stars emerge across the night sky. Quietly he whispered:

"God, give me strength to face pain." "Give me courage to speak truth." "Give me wisdom to receive help."

For the first time in years, David felt movement toward freedom rather than movement away from pain.
