CHAPTER FOUR

The Rebuke of Love

A week later Aaron returned to David's house carrying a notebook beneath his arm. Aaron stepped inside without hesitation. Familiarity removed the need for formal invitations.

"You've been quiet," Aaron said. "I know you're wrestling with something." "Sitting alone won't fix any of this."

David looked down toward his hands. "I'm trying." "I just don't know what else to do."

Aaron placed the notebook on the table. "I made a list."

David raised an eyebrow. "A list?"

Aaron nodded. "Everything you've been hiding." "The injuries." "The stress." "The family tension." "Every burden you've carried by yourself."

"You need to stop pretending you're okay."

David hesitated before reaching toward the notebook. Aaron opened pages and began reading.

"You're exhausted from the accident." "You ignored medical advice." "You're emotionally drained." "You're carrying pride and shame like armor." "You need help."

David swallowed hard. "You don't understand." "I can't just let everything go."

Aaron's voice remained calm. "I understand more than you think." "Pretending keeps pain trapped."

"Open rebuke is better than secret love."

"I care too much to watch silence destroy you."

Part of David wanted to argue. Part of him wanted to push the notebook away. Part of him wanted familiar walls back.

Yet concern filled Aaron's words. Steady. Honest. Unshaken.

David looked downward. "I don't want weakness."

Aaron leaned forward. "You're human." "Strength doesn't hide pain." "Strength faces pain."

Silence settled again. Different silence. Gentler silence.

David picked up the pen. Slowly he began writing. Physical injuries. Lonely nights. Fear. Shame. Arguments. Hidden wounds.

Every sentence felt like release. Every word removed another layer of weight.

"Truth doesn't destroy you." "It frees you."

Aaron smiled. "Now comes the difficult part." "Boundaries." "Healing." "Receiving help."

David nodded slowly. Hope flickered inside him. Small. Fragile. Real.
