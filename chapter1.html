CHAPTER ONE

The Weight He Wouldn't Name

David stood in the dim light of his bathroom, staring at a reflection that no longer felt like his own. His jaw was tight, his shoulders tense, and the faint bruise along his ribs reminded him of the accident at work he had tried to ignore.

Pain radiated through his side with every breath, but he told himself the same thing he always did.

"You're fine. You can handle it."

He pulled on his shirt slowly, wincing as the fabric brushed against his skin. Outside, the world was waking up. Inside, he felt like he was sinking.

The pressure from work, the silence at home, and the weight of expectations pressed heavily on his chest. He could lift boxes and carry beams all day, yet the burden in his heart felt far heavier.

When his friend Aaron called that evening, David almost ignored the phone. Questions felt exhausting. Sympathy felt unbearable. Aaron remained persistent and something in his voice broke through David's resistance.

"I've noticed you're not yourself," Aaron said quietly. "You're limping at work. You don't laugh like you used to. What's really going on?"

David hesitated. His throat tightened. Words fought their way upward. "I'm fine. Just a little sore."

Aaron paused. "No, you're not fine. You're hurting. Pretending doesn't make pain disappear."

Those words struck harder than physical injury. David wanted to end the conversation. Silence felt safer. Yet another feeling rose beneath fear. Truth.

For the first time in a long while, someone looked beyond strength. Someone saw pain. Someone stayed.

David sat quietly and wondered what might happen if hiding finally ended.
