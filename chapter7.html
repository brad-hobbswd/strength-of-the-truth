CHAPTER SEVEN

Seeds of Strength

Spring arrived with quiet changes. David felt stronger physically, steadier emotionally, and clearer spiritually. Healing had reshaped something deep inside him.

Another realization slowly emerged. Healing was never meant for David alone. Lessons learned through pain carried purpose beyond personal recovery.

Truth. Courage. Honesty. Freedom. Every lesson now felt like something meant to be shared.

Everything started through small moments. At work David noticed a younger coworker named Liam struggling beneath the pressure of a demanding project. Liam looked familiar. Quiet. Overworked. Exhausted. Hidden pain sat behind tired eyes.

One afternoon David approached him carefully.

"Hey Liam." "I've noticed things seem heavy lately." "Are you okay?"

Liam looked surprised. Almost uncomfortable.

"I'm fine."

David smiled gently. Words felt familiar. Too familiar.

"You don't have to pretend with me."

"I've stood where you're standing." "I thought strength meant carrying everything alone." "I learned something different."

Weeks passed. Conversations continued. Trust slowly grew. David shared pieces of his own journey. The accident. The loneliness. The fear. The months spent pretending.

Eventually Liam began opening hidden places inside his own life. Worries surfaced. Pressures surfaced. Pain surfaced.

David listened. Encouraged. Guided. Not through perfection. Through honesty.

Changes spread beyond work. David's father noticed differences too. Conversations became deeper. Walls slowly lowered. Truth opened places silence never could.

David realized mentoring required very little. Not perfection. Not expertise. Only honesty. Empathy. Courage to speak truth through love.

One evening while walking through a neighborhood park, David noticed a group of young men gathered around a basketball court. Laughter filled the air. Yet tiredness sat behind several smiles. David recognized those eyes. He once carried that same weariness.

He walked toward them.

"Mind giving me a minute?"

Curious faces turned. David shared his story. Bruises. Pain. Silence. Fear. Aaron. Truth. Healing.

Silence followed afterward. Different silence. Thoughtful silence.

One young man finally spoke. Voice shaking.

"I think I've been hiding too." "I just don't know where to begin."

David smiled warmly.

"Begin with truth." "One step at a time." "You don't have to walk alone."

Walking home later that night felt different. Pain no longer looked meaningless. Pain had become soil. Truth had become seed. Healing had become something capable of growing in others.

David whispered gratitude beneath the evening sky. Healing changed his life. Helping others changed his heart.
