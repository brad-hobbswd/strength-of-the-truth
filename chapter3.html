CHAPTER THREE

When the Mirror Hurts

Morning light slipped through the blinds and brushed across the mirror in David's room. David caught his reflection while getting dressed for work, though this time his eyes remained fixed.

The man staring back looked tired. Worn. Older than expected. Bruises had begun fading, yet another ache remained untouched.

"You're fine."

David whispered familiar words beneath his breath. Words repeated countless times. Words now sounding hollow. Empty.

Throughout the workday, his thoughts drifted. Machinery echoed. Tools struck steel. Coworkers talked nearby. None of those sounds felt louder than Aaron's words.

"Maybe strength isn't silence."

Evening eventually arrived. David returned home and ignored familiar routines. Television remained dark. Distractions stayed untouched. Silence filled the room. David sat alone. Thinking. Remembering.

Memories rose like waves. His father telling him to man up after childhood tears. Pain dismissed. Emotions buried. Years spent hiding fear beneath work and toughness.

David stood and slowly walked toward the mirror. Eyes met reflection. Questions surfaced.

"Why do you keep pretending?"

No answer came. Only truth.

Pride once felt protective. Silence once felt powerful. Now both felt like prison walls.

A phone vibrated nearby. Aaron. David answered.

"Hey." "Checking on you."

Silence followed. David swallowed hard. Then finally spoke.

"I'm not okay."

Words felt heavy. Yet strangely freeing.

"I keep trying to be strong." "I don't even know what strength means anymore."

Aaron responded gently. "That isn't the end." "That's the beginning." "When truth becomes visible, freedom begins."

Tears filled David's eyes. This time nothing held them back. Nothing hidden. Nothing buried.

David stared into the mirror again. Weakness no longer looked back. Honesty did.

Late that night David whispered a prayer for the first time in years.

"God... I'm tired of pretending." "Help me stop hiding."

The room remained quiet afterward. Yet quiet no longer felt empty. Deep inside, something small and fragile began growing. Peace.
